About Colombia

History & Geography

Colombia's story spans from ancient indigenous civilizations to Spanish colonization and independence. Today, it stands as one of South America's most diverse nations, bordered by Panama, Venezuela, Brazil, Peru, and Ecuador.

With territories spanning across the Amazon rainforest, Andean highlands, Caribbean and Pacific coastlines, and vast plains, Colombia hosts an incredible variety of ecosystems in one country.

Colombian Gastronomy

Bandeja Paisa

Antioquia Region

Colombia's most iconic dish featuring beans, rice, ground meat, chorizo, fried egg, plantain, avocado, and arepa. A hearty meal that represents the Paisa culture.

Ajiaco Santafereño

Bogotá & Andean Region

A hearty soup made with three varieties of potatoes, corn, chicken, and a native herb called guascas. Served with cream, capers, and avocado on the side.

Arepas

Nationwide

These corn cakes are a staple of Colombian cuisine. Made from ground maize dough and served in various styles across the country - plain, filled with cheese, eggs, or meat.

Caribbean Seafood

Caribbean Coast

Fresh fish and seafood prepared with coconut milk, plantains, and tropical seasonings. Try the famous cazuela de mariscos (seafood stew) or fried mojarra fish.

Tropical Fruit Juices

Nationwide

Colombia's biodiversity means an incredible variety of exotic fruits. Try refreshing juices made from lulo, maracuyá (passion fruit), guanábana (soursop), or zapote.

Empanadas

Nationwide

Golden fried pastries filled with meat, potatoes, or cheese. Each region has its own version, typically served with spicy ají sauce on the side.

5 Unmissable Destinations

Caño Cristales

Known as "The River of Five Colors" or "The Liquid Rainbow," this stunning river in the Serranía de la Macarena National Park displays spectacular colors due to unique plants growing on the riverbed.

View more

Ciudad Perdida

This archaeological site in the Sierra Nevada de Santa Marta mountains predates Machu Picchu by 650 years. The multi-day trek takes you through lush jungle to the ancient Tayrona civilization ruins.

View more

Uramba Bahía Málaga

This Pacific coast national park is famous for the seasonal migration of humpback whales. Visit between July and November to witness these majestic creatures in their breeding grounds.

View more

Isla de los Micos

Located in the Amazon rainforest near Leticia, this island is home to hundreds of squirrel monkeys that roam freely. Visitors can interact with these friendly creatures in their natural habitat.

Cabo de la Vela

This remote area in the La Guajira peninsula offers otherworldly landscapes where desert meets the Caribbean Sea. Experience Wayuu indigenous culture, stunning beaches, and spectacular sunsets.

How to Get There

Colombia has multiple international airports with direct flights from major cities worldwide. The main international gateways are:

  • El Dorado International Airport (Bogotá)
  • José María Córdova International Airport (Medellín)
  • Rafael Núñez International Airport (Cartagena)
  • Alfonso Bonilla Aragón International Airport (Cali)

Major airlines serving Colombia include Avianca, LATAM, American Airlines, Delta, United, Air France, Iberia, and more.

Once in Colombia, you have several options to travel between cities and regions:

  • Domestic Flights: The quickest way to cover long distances. Avianca, LATAM, and Viva Air offer extensive domestic networks.
  • Buses: Comfortable and affordable long-distance buses connect major cities.
  • Taxis and Ride-sharing: Widely available in cities, with Uber and similar apps operating in major urban areas.
  • Car Rentals: Available in major cities, though driving in Colombia can be challenging in certain areas.

Entry Requirements & Health

Visitors from many countries can enter Colombia without a visa for up to 90 days, including travelers from the USA, Canada, EU countries, Australia, and most Latin American nations.

You'll need:

  • A passport valid for at least 6 months beyond your stay
  • Return/onward ticket
  • Proof of accommodation

Check with the Colombian consulate in your country for the most current information.

Recommended vaccinations for Colombia include:

  • Yellow Fever (required if you're entering from a country with risk of yellow fever)
  • Hepatitis A & B
  • Typhoid
  • Tetanus-diphtheria
  • Measles-mumps-rubella (MMR)

Malaria prevention is recommended if visiting rural areas, especially in the Amazon, Pacific coast, or certain lowland regions.

Always consult your healthcare provider or a travel medicine specialist before your trip.

Safety & Recommendations

Colombia has become much safer for tourists in recent years, but as with any destination, it's important to take basic precautions:

  • Stick to tourist areas and ask locals or hotel staff about areas to avoid
  • Use official taxis or ride-sharing apps rather than hailing street cabs
  • Keep valuables secure and avoid displaying expensive items
  • Be cautious with ATM usage and carry only necessary amounts of cash
  • Keep a photocopy of your passport and store the original in your hotel safe
  • Register with your country's embassy or consulate when you arrive

  • Learn basic Spanish phrases - Colombians appreciate when visitors try to speak their language
  • Respect local customs and traditions, especially in indigenous areas
  • Drink bottled water in most areas
  • Purchase travel insurance that includes medical coverage and emergency evacuation
  • Be aware that altitude sickness can affect visitors to Bogotá and other high-altitude areas
  • Bring sunscreen and insect repellent, especially when visiting tropical areas
